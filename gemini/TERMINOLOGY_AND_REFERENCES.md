### Water Utility Operations

| English | Indonesian | Definition |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Non-Revenue Water (NRW) | Air Hilang (AH) | Water produced but not billed (physical loss + commercial loss) |

| Commercial Loss | Kehilangan Komersial | Water lost due to billing/metering issues (illegal connections, theft, meter errors) |
| District Metering Area (DMA) | Area Meter Distrik | Defined area with single entry/exit point for monitoring and leak localization |
| Pressure Reducing Valve (PRV) | Katup Pengurangan Tekanan | Device that maintains optimal pressure to minimize leaks |
| Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) | Infrastruktur Meter Pintar | Smart meters with remote reading and data transmission |
| SCADA | Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition | Real-time monitoring system for water production, distribution, pressure |
| Leak Detection | Deteksi Kebocoran | Acoustic or pressure-based methods to locate leaks |
| Meter Reading | Pembacaan Meter | Collecting water consumption data from customer meters |
| Billing System | Sistem Penagihan | Software for recording consumption and generating customer invoices |
| Service Connection | Sambungan Layanan | Physical pipe and meter connecting distribution network to customer premises |

## External References & Resources

### Indonesian Legal Framework

**Essential Laws & Regulations:**

1. **Law No. 11 of 1974** (Undang-Undang No. 11 Tahun 1974)
   - Basic law governing water supply in Indonesia
   - Establishes PDAM as water utility provider
   - Available: https://www.bpkp.go.id/

2. **Law No. 30 of 2009** (Undang-Undang No. 30 Tahun 2009)
   - Personal Data Protection Law
   - Regulations: https://www.ojk.go.id/
   - Key requirement: Privacy impact assessment, data breach notification

3. **Law No. 8 of 1997** (Undang-Undang No. 8 Tahun 1997)
   - Competition Law
   - Prevents monopolistic practices
   - Relevant for PDAM service pricing and regional competition

4. **Government Regulation No. 6 of 2017** (Peraturan Pemerintah No. 6 Tahun 2017)
   - State-Owned Enterprise Governance
   - Applies to PDAM as state/regional enterprises
   - Defines governance requirements, reporting standards

### Cybersecurity & Information Security Standards

**International Standards (Applicable in Indonesia):**

1. **ISO/IEC 27001** — Information Security Management System
   - Framework for establishing information security program
   - Certification available
   - Aligns with BSSN guidelines

2. **NIST Cybersecurity Framework** (U.S. Standard, internationally recognized)
   - Functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover
   - Used as reference for critical infrastructure security
   - Available: https://www.nist.gov/

3. **BSSN Guidelines** (Indonesian Standard)
   - Specifically for critical infrastructure including water utilities
   - Mandatory compliance for government-owned water companies
   - Available: https://bssn.go.id/

4. **PCI-DSS** (Payment Card Industry - Data Security Standard)
   - Relevant if PDAM accepts credit card payments
   - Defines data protection requirements for payment systems

### Governance Frameworks

**COBIT (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technology)**
   - Industry standard for IT governance
   - Published by ISACA
   - Available: https://www.isaca.org/resources/cobit
   - Version 5 most common; COBIT 2019 latest

**ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library)**
   - Best practices for IT service management
   - Defines service management processes (incident, change, capacity)
   - Available: https://www.axelos.com/certifications/itil-certifications
   - Often used alongside COBIT for operational excellence

**PMI PMBOK (Project Management Institute - Project Management Body of Knowledge)**
   - Project management standard
   - Relevant for transformation program management
   - Available: https://www.pmi.org/

## Communication Examples

### How to Reference Different Audiences

**When talking to PDAM Directors/Board:**
- Use business impact terminology: "revenue impact," "operational efficiency," "stakeholder confidence"
- Reference regulatory requirements: "PDP compliance," "BPK audit findings," "BSSN standards"
- Focus on strategic alignment: "business strategy," "long-term value," "risk mitigation"

**When talking to IT/Operations staff:**
- Use technical terminology: "system integration," "API," "cloud migration," "security posture"
- Discuss implementation details: "go-live," "cutover," "rollback," "performance tuning"
- Address practical concerns: "support," "training," "documentation," "SLA"

**When talking to Vendors:**
- Use formal language: "requirements," "specifications," "deliverables," "acceptance criteria"
- Reference standards: "COBIT," "ISO/IEC," "ITIL," "project management methodology"
- Discuss economics: "TCO," "ROI," "implementation timeline," "support model"

**When talking to Regulators/Auditors:**
- Use compliance language: "compliance framework," "control objective," "risk assessment," "audit findings"
- Reference laws and standards: "PDP," "BSSN," "BPK," "COBIT"
- Discuss governance: "oversight," "accountability," "transparency," "stakeholder reporting"
